MARILYN MONBROE

COLLECTION: POP ART

The Warhol Marilyn series is about the mechanical reproduction of an icon — the same face, repeated, flattened, color-shifted until the person disappears into the image. BasicGlitch takes that logic one step further: replace the face with Broboticus in a pleated skirt throwing double rock horns, and alternate with panels of oversized multicolor lips that could belong to either of them. The six-panel grid runs across blue, yellow, lime green, lavender, purple, and red — Warhol's palette, Warhol's format, Warhol's argument about what fame does to a face. Except the face is a BROBOTICUS chest panel. The lips are whoever's lips you need them to be. The title is perfect.
