DUST TO DUST

COLLECTION: SURREALISM

If Gaia of the Wasteland is the last act of defiance, Dust to Dust is the moment just before it. This figure is also rooted — her torso rising from gnarled tree roots gripping dry earth — but where Gaia's posture is still, this one is in motion, her wind-caught hair streaked with branches and foliage that may be growing or may be scattering. She looks directly outward. The moss on her shoulders, the sun low and orange behind a dead tree, the cracked ground identical to her companion piece — these two works share a world. Shown together they tell a fuller story about what Earth looks like when it remembers it is alive, and what it looks like when it isn't sure.
